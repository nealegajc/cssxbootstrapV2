Mac Miller Discography
Album: K.I.D.S. (Kickin' Incredibly Dope Shit)
Year: 2010
Type: Mixtape
Tracks: 16 tracks
Description: Mac Miller's breakthrough mixtape that put him on the map. A youthful, energetic project showcasing his early style with catchy hooks and party anthems. Featured hits like "Nikes on My Feet" and "Senior Skip Day."
Album: Best Day Ever
Year: 2011
Type: Mixtape
Tracks: 16 tracks
Description: A celebratory mixtape that showcases Mac's optimistic outlook and party-ready anthems. Features the hit single "Best Day Ever" and demonstrates his growing confidence as an artist with improved production and flow.
Album: I Love Life, Thank You
Year: 2011
Type: Mixtape
Tracks: 13 tracks
Description: A gratitude-filled mixtape reflecting Mac's appreciation for life and his growing success. Features more mature themes while maintaining his signature upbeat energy and includes collaborations with various artists.
Album: Blue Slide Park
Year: 2011
Type: Studio Album
Tracks: 16 tracks
Description: Mac's debut studio album that debuted at #1 on the Billboard 200. Named after a playground in Pittsburgh, this album captures his hometown pride and features more polished production with tracks like "Party on Fifth Ave."
Album: Macadelic
Year: 2012
Type: Mixtape
Tracks: 17 tracks
Description: A psychedelic and experimental mixtape that marked a significant artistic shift for Mac. Features darker, more introspective themes with drug references and showcases his evolution from party rap to more complex, atmospheric production.
Album: Watching Movies with the Sound Off
Year: 2013
Type: Studio Album
Tracks: 19 tracks
Description: A darker, more introspective album marking Mac's artistic evolution. Features experimental production and deeper lyrical content, with standout tracks like "Objects in the Mirror" and collaborations with Earl Sweatshirt.
Album: Faces
Year: 2014 / 2021
Type: Mixtape
Tracks: 24 tracks
Description: Originally released as a free mixtape, later officially released on streaming platforms. A dark, introspective project exploring Mac's struggles with addiction. Contains fan favorites like "Diablo" and "Grand Finale."
Album: GO:OD AM
Year: 2015
Type: Studio Album
Tracks: 17 tracks
Description: Mac's third studio album explores themes of addiction and recovery. Features a mix of upbeat tracks and somber reflections, including hits like "100 Grandkids" and "Weekend" featuring Miguel.
Album: The Divine Feminine
Year: 2016
Type: Studio Album
Tracks: 10 tracks
Description: A love letter to femininity and relationships, this album showcases Mac's versatility with jazz and R&B influences. Features collaborations with Ariana Grande, Anderson .Paak, and Kali Uchis.
Album: Swimming
Year: 2018
Type: Studio Album
Tracks: 13 tracks
Description: Mac's final studio album released during his lifetime. A deeply personal and emotional project dealing with mental health, love, and loss. Features hits like "Self Care" and "Ladders."
Album: Circles
Year: 2020
Type: Posthumous Album
Tracks: 12 tracks
Description: A posthumous release that serves as a companion to "Swimming." Completed by producer Jon Brion, this album showcases Mac's softer, more melodic side with beautiful arrangements and heartfelt lyrics.
Album: Balloonerism
Year: 2025
Type: Posthumous Album
Tracks: 14 tracks
Description: A posthumous release featuring previously unreleased material from Mac Miller's archives. This album showcases Mac's experimental side with psychedelic influences and introspective lyrics, offering fans a glimpse into his creative process during a pivotal period.
